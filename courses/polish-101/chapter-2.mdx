# Party and Social Gathering Phrases

## Introduction
Polish social gatherings are lively, warm, and full of conversation! Whether it's a casual house party, birthday celebration, or family dinner, having a few key phrases will help you feel included and connect with Polish speakers. This chapter focuses on practical expressions that will help you navigate social situations with confidence.

## Greeting Friends and Family

### Casual Greetings
- **"Cześć!"** (cheshch) - Hi/Hello
- **"Hej!"** (hey) - Hey
- **"Siema!"** (sheh-ma) - Hey/What's up (very casual, used among friends)
- **"Co słychać?"** (tso swih-hach) - What's up?/What's new?

### Formal Greetings
- **"Dzień dobry"** (jean doh-brih) - Good day/Hello (before 6 PM)
- **"Dobry wieczór"** (doh-brih vyeh-choor) - Good evening
- **"Witam wszystkich"** (vee-tam vshist-kich) - Welcome everyone

### Who to Greet Formally vs. Casually
In Polish culture, it's important to know when to use formal vs. casual greetings:

- **Use formal with**: your partner's parents, elderly people, your partner's boss, people you're meeting for the first time
- **Use casual with**: friends of your age, your partner's siblings/cousins who are close to your age, children

## Small Talk and Icebreakers

### Starting Conversations
- **"Jak się nazywasz?"** (yak sheh nah-zih-vash) - What's your name?
- **"Skąd jesteś?"** (skond yes-tesh) - Where are you from?
- **"Jak długo uczysz się polskiego?"** (yak dwoo-go oo-chish sheh pol-skye-go) - How long have you been learning Polish?
- **"Czy to twój pierwszy raz w Polsce?"** (chi to tvuy pyerv-shi ras v pol-sche) - Is this your first time in Poland?

### Responding to Common Questions
- **"Jestem z..."** (yes-tem z...) - I'm from...
- **"Uczę się polskiego od..."** (oo-cheh sheh pol-skye-go od...) - I've been learning Polish for...
- **"Mówię trochę po polsku"** (moo-vyeh tro-heh po pol-skoo) - I speak a little Polish
- **"Dopiero zaczynam"** (do-pye-ro za-chi-nam) - I'm just starting

## Toast Phrases and Drinking Customs

### Making a Toast
- **"Na zdrowie!"** (na zdro-vyeh) - Cheers!/To health!
- **"Sto lat!"** (sto lat) - A hundred years! (common birthday toast)
- **"Za nas!"** (za nas) - To us!
- **"Wszystkiego najlepszego!"** (vshist-kye-go nay-lep-sheh-go) - All the best!

### Drinking Custom Tips
1. Wait for everyone to receive their drinks before drinking
2. Look people in the eyes when clinking glasses
3. If someone makes a toast, everyone should take at least a small sip
4. Never refill your own glass without offering to refill others first

## Polite Phrases for Social Navigation

### Excusing Yourself
- **"Przepraszam"** (psheh-pra-sham) - Excuse me/I'm sorry
- **"Muszę iść do łazienki"** (moo-sheh ish-ch do wa-zyen-kee) - I need to go to the bathroom
- **"Zaraz wracam"** (za-raz vra-tsam) - I'll be right back
- **"Potrzebuję chwilę na świeżym powietrzu"** (po-cheh-boo-yeh hvi-leh na shvye-zhim po-vyet-shoo) - I need a moment of fresh air

### Asking for Help
- **"Czy możesz mi pomóc?"** (chi mo-zhesh mee po-muts) - Can you help me?
- **"Gdzie jest..."** (g-jeh yest...) - Where is...
- **"Nie rozumiem, czy możesz powtórzyć?"** (nyeh ro-zoo-myem, chi mo-zhesh pov-tu-zhich) - I don't understand, can you repeat?
- **"Mówisz po angielsku?"** (moo-vish po an-gyel-skoo) - Do you speak English?

## Party and Event Vocabulary

### Common Party Words
- **impreza** (im-pre-za) - party
- **urodziny** (oo-ro-jee-ni) - birthday
- **rocznica** (roch-nee-tsa) - anniversary
- **spotkanie** (spot-ka-nyeh) - meeting/gathering
- **grill** (gril) - barbecue
- **sylwester** (sil-ves-ter) - New Year's Eve

### Useful Event Phrases
- **"O której kończy się impreza?"** (o ktu-rey kon-chi sheh im-pre-za) - When does the party end?
- **"Czy mogę coś przynieść?"** (chi mo-geh tsosh pshi-nyesh-ch) - Can I bring something?
- **"To świetna impreza!"** (to shvyet-na im-pre-za) - This is a great party!
- **"Czy mogę przyjść z partnerem/partnerką?"** (chi mo-geh pshi-ish-ch z part-ne-rem/part-ner-kom) - Can I come with my partner?

## Practical Exercise: Party Conversation Practice

Let's practice a simple conversation you might have at a Polish gathering:

### Scenario: Meeting a friend of your partner at a party

1. **Greet them**: "Cześć! Jestem [your name]." (Hi! I'm [your name])

2. **Introduce yourself**: "Jestem partnerem/partnerką [your partner's name]." (I'm [your partner's name]'s partner)

3. **Ask a question**: "Jak długo znasz [your partner's name]?" (How long have you known [your partner's name]?)

4. **Talk about learning Polish**: "Uczę się polskiego. To trudny język!" (I'm learning Polish. It's a difficult language!)

5. **Make a toast**: "Na zdrowie! Miło cię poznać!" (Cheers! Nice to meet you!)

Practice this conversation with your partner playing different roles. Try to respond naturally, and don't worry if you need to mix in some English!
