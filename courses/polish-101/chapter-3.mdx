# Food, Drinks, and Compliments

## Introduction
Food is central to Polish culture and social gatherings! Polish hosts take immense pride in preparing traditional dishes and ensuring guests are well-fed. In this chapter, you'll learn essential food vocabulary, how to compliment the cook, express your preferences, and understand Polish hospitality customs. These phrases will help you navigate meals with your partner's family and friends while showing genuine appreciation for their culinary efforts.

## Essential Food Vocabulary

### Traditional Polish Dishes
- **pierogi** (pye-ro-gee) - filled dumplings with various stuffings
- **bigos** (bee-gos) - hunter's stew with sauerkraut and meat
- **żurek** (zhoo-rek) - sour rye soup, often with sausage and egg
- **kotlet schabowy** (kot-let scha-bo-vih) - breaded pork cutlet
- **gołąbki** (go-womb-kee) - cabbage rolls stuffed with meat and rice
- **placki ziemniaczane** (plats-kee zyem-nya-cha-neh) - potato pancakes
- **kiełbasa** (kyew-ba-sa) - Polish sausage
- **kluski śląskie** (kloos-kee shlons-kyeh) - Silesian dumplings
- **kapusta** (ka-poos-ta) - sauerkraut/cabbage dishes

### Common Ingredients and Side Dishes
- **ziemniaki** (zyem-nya-kee) - potatoes
- **chleb** (hlep) - bread
- **ogórki kiszone** (o-goor-kee kee-sho-neh) - pickled cucumbers
- **śmietana** (shmye-ta-na) - sour cream
- **buraki** (boo-ra-kee) - beets
- **grzyby** (g-zhih-bih) - mushrooms
- **kapusta kiszona** (ka-poos-ta kee-sho-na) - sauerkraut
- **ser** (ser) - cheese
- **miód** (myood) - honey

### Desserts and Sweets
- **sernik** (ser-neek) - cheesecake
- **makowiec** (ma-ko-vyets) - poppy seed roll
- **szarlotka** (shar-lot-ka) - apple cake
- **pączki** (ponch-kee) - Polish donuts
- **faworki/chrust** (fa-vor-kee/hroost) - Angel wings (crispy pastry)
- **pierniki** (pyer-nee-kee) - gingerbread cookies
- **kisiel** (kee-shel) - fruit jelly dessert

## Drinks and Beverages

### Alcoholic Drinks
- **piwo** (pee-vo) - beer
- **wódka** (vood-ka) - vodka
- **nalewka** (na-lef-ka) - fruit liqueur
- **wino** (vee-no) - wine
- **miód pitny** (myood peet-nih) - mead

### Non-Alcoholic Drinks
- **herbata** (her-ba-ta) - tea
- **kawa** (ka-va) - coffee
- **sok** (sok) - juice
- **woda** (vo-da) - water
- **kompot** (kom-pot) - fruit compote

## Complimenting the Food and Host

### Food Compliments
- **"Bardzo smaczne!"** (bar-dzo smach-neh) - Very tasty!
- **"Pyszne!"** (pish-neh) - Delicious!
- **"Świetnie gotujesz!"** (shvyet-nyeh go-too-yesh) - You cook wonderfully!
- **"Palce lizać!"** (pal-tseh lee-zach) - Finger-licking good! (Literally: "lick your fingers")
- **"Najlepsze jakie jadłem/jadłam!"** (nay-lep-sheh ya-kyeh yad-wem/yad-wam) - The best I've ever eaten!

### Host Compliments
- **"Dziękuję za gościnę"** (jen-koo-yeh za gosh-chee-neh) - Thank you for your hospitality
- **"Doceniam twój wysiłek"** (do-tseh-nyam tvuy vih-see-wek) - I appreciate your effort
- **"Wszystko jest wspaniałe"** (vshist-ko yest vs-pa-nya-weh) - Everything is wonderful
- **"Czuję się jak w domu"** (choo-yeh sheh yak v do-moo) - I feel at home

## Expressing Preferences and Dietary Needs

### Expressing Likes
- **"Uwielbiam..."** (oo-vyel-byam) - I love...
- **"Moje ulubione to..."** (mo-yeh oo-loo-byo-neh to) - My favorite is...
- **"Chętnie spróbuję więcej"** (hent-nyeh sproo-boo-yeh vyent-sey) - I'd gladly try more

### Expressing Dislikes (Politely)
- **"Jestem pełny/pełna"** (yes-tem pew-nih/pew-na) - I'm full (a polite way to decline more food)
- **"Dziękuję, wystarczy"** (jen-koo-yeh, vih-star-chi) - Thank you, it's enough
- **"To trochę za ostre dla mnie"** (to tro-heh za os-treh dla mnyeh) - This is a bit too spicy for me

### Dietary Restrictions
- **"Jestem wegetarianinem/wegetarianką"** (yes-tem ve-ge-ta-rya-nee-nem/ve-ge-ta-ryan-kom) - I'm vegetarian
- **"Nie jem..."** (nyeh yem) - I don't eat...
- **"Jestem uczulony/uczulona na..."** (yes-tem oo-choo-lo-nih/oo-choo-lo-na na) - I'm allergic to...
- **"Czy to zawiera gluten/laktozę?"** (chi to za-vye-ra gloo-ten/lak-to-zeh) - Does this contain gluten/lactose?

## Understanding Polish Hospitality Customs

### Important Cultural Notes
1. **Refusing food can be seen as impolite** - Polish hosts often insist you eat more. A polite "Jestem pełny/pełna" (I'm full) with a pat on your stomach usually works.

2. **Second helpings are expected** - Hosts will likely offer you more food. Taking a small second portion is considered a compliment to the cook.

3. **Bread has special status** - Never throw away bread or place it upside down. If bread falls on the floor, tradition says to pick it up and kiss it.

4. **Toasts are important** - Wait for the host to make the first toast before drinking alcohol.

5. **Offering to help** - Asking "Czy mogę w czymś pomóc?" (Can I help with anything?) is appreciated but might be declined. Insist gently if you really want to help.

6. **Bringing something small** - When invited to someone's home, it's customary to bring a small gift (flowers, wine, or chocolates).

## Practical Exercise: Restaurant and Dinner Scenarios

Let's practice some essential dialogue for dining situations:

### Scenario 1: At a Polish Restaurant

1. **Ordering food**: "Poproszę pierogi z kapustą i grzybami." (I'd like pierogi with cabbage and mushrooms)

2. **Asking about a dish**: "Co to jest żurek?" (What is żurek?)

3. **Asking for the bill**: "Poproszę rachunek." (The bill, please)

4. **Complimenting the meal**: "Posiłek był wspaniały." (The meal was wonderful)

### Scenario 2: At Your Partner's Family Dinner

1. **Complimenting the cook**: "Ten bigos jest pyszny! Czy możesz podzielić się przepisem?" (This bigos is delicious! Can you share the recipe?)

2. **Politely declining more food**: "Dziękuję, było przepyszne, ale jestem już pełny/pełna." (Thank you, it was delicious, but I'm already full)

3. **Offering to help clean up**: "Czy mogę pomóc pozmywać naczynia?" (Can I help wash the dishes?)

4. **Thanking the host**: "Dziękuję za wspaniały wieczór i pyszne jedzenie." (Thank you for the wonderful evening and delicious food)
